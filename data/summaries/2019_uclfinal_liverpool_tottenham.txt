Liverpool 2–0 Tottenham Hotspur
2019 UEFA Champions League Final, Wanda Metropolitano, Madrid – 1 June 2019

Liverpool claimed their sixth Champions League title with a 2–0 victory in the all-English final against Tottenham Hotspur in Madrid, dominating crucial moments of the match and converting key opportunities.

Liverpool struck early when Mohamed Salah converted a penalty in the 2nd minute after Moussa Sissoko handled the ball inside the area following a cross from Sadio Mané, giving Liverpool a swift advantage and setting the tone for the final.

Much of the first half saw Tottenham attempt to gain a foothold, but Liverpool’s defence and goalkeeper Alisson absorbed pressure and maintained the clean sheet. The match remained 1–0 through most of the second half as both sides traded possession and chances without further breakthroughs.

The decisive second goal arrived late. In the 87th minute, Divock Origi scored with a low finish into the corner after sustained attacking play, sealing the win and securing the trophy for Liverpool in front of fans from both sides.

Key events:

2′ – Mohamed Salah (Liverpool) penalty goal.

87′ – Divock Origi (Liverpool) goal.

Liverpool lifted the Champions League trophy for the sixth time, while Tottenham remained in search of their first major European title.
