Brazil 4–1 South Korea
2022 FIFA World Cup – Round of 16, Stadium 974, Ras Abu Aboud – 5 December 2022

Brazil began brightly, taking the lead in the 7th minute when Vinícius Júnior fired into the corner after a build-up down the left flank. Brazil’s attacking momentum continued, and they were awarded a penalty in the 13th minute, which Neymar converted with a low finish into the corner to make it 2–0. The hosts dominated possession and chances throughout the first half.

Brazil extended their advantage in the 29th minute with a composed finish from Richarlison after good link-up play in the box, and shortly thereafter in the 36th minute, Lucas Paquetá volleyed home from close range following a clipped cross. At half-time, Brazil led 4–0.

South Korea pulled one back in the 76th minute through Paik Seung-Ho, who unleashed a powerful long-range strike that beat the goalkeeper and provided a consolation goal, but Brazil maintained control for the rest of the match to secure a 4–1 victory and advance to the quarter-finals.

Key events:

7′ – Vinícius Júnior (Brazil) goal.

13′ – Neymar (Brazil) penalty.

29′ – Richarlison (Brazil) goal.

36′ – Lucas Paquetá (Brazil) goal.

76′ – Paik Seung-Ho (South Korea) goal.

Brazil’s comprehensive attacking display, especially in the first half, proved decisive in this knockout round tie.
