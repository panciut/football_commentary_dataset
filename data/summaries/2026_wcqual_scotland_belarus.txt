Scotland 2–1 Belarus
2026 FIFA World Cup Qualifying – UEFA Group C, Hampden Park, Glasgow – 12 October 2025

Scotland secured a hard-fought 2–1 victory over Belarus in their World Cup qualifying fixture at Hampden Park. Che Adams opened the scoring in the 15th minute, firing low into the net after a build-up attack gave Scotland the early lead. Belarus struggled to create clear chances but remained competitive throughout the first half.  ￼

After a relatively quiet second period, Scott McTominay extended Scotland’s advantage in the 84th minute, finishing from close range following good interplay and sustained pressure from the hosts. Belarus responded in stoppage time when Gleb Kuczko scored in the 90+6th minute, pulling one back, but it proved only a consolation as Scotland held on for the win.  ￼

The match was closely contested, with Scotland’s defence and goalkeeper making key interventions to maintain their lead despite Belarus pressuring late on.  ￼
