Spain 2–1 England
UEFA Euro 2024 Final, Olympiastadion, Berlin – 14 July 2024

Spain secured the UEFA European Championship title with a late-deciding victory over England in the final at Berlin’s Olympic Stadium. After a goalless first half where both sides had limited clear chances, Nico Williams opened the scoring in the 47th minute with a low finish after a penetrating move that began with fellow wide player Lamine Yamal. Spain’s early second-half push gave them control as they sought to break the deadlock.  ￼

England responded in the 73rd minute when substitute Cole Palmer struck from outside the box following a lay-off from Jude Bellingham, levelling the score and giving the Three Lions renewed belief.  ￼

With the match heading toward extra time, Mikel Oyarzabal struck the winning goal in the 86th minute. He met a low cross from Marc Cucurella and slotted home from close range, turning the game in Spain’s favour and securing a 2–1 victory.  ￼
