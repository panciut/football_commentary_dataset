Colombia 1–1 England (England win 4–3 on penalties)
2018 FIFA World Cup – Round of 16, Spartak Stadium, Moscow – 3 July 2018

England took the lead in the 57th minute when Harry Kane converted a penalty after being fouled during a corner situation, giving his side the advantage in a tightly contested knockout match.

As the game approached full time, Colombia struck back. In the 90+3rd minute, Yerry Mina rose highest from a late corner to head home an equaliser, taking the match into extra time after a period of sustained pressure from Colombia.

Extra time saw few clear chances for either side, and neither team could find a winner in the additional 30 minutes, leading to a penalty shoot-out to decide the tie.

In the shoot-out, England emerged victorious 4–3. Colombia’s Mateus Uribe saw his penalty hit the crossbar, and goalkeeper Jordan Pickford made a vital save from Carlos Bacca’s effort. Eric Dier converted the decisive penalty to send England through to the quarter-finals.
