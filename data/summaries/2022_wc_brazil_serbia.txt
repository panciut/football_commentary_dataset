Brazil 2–0 Serbia
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group G, Lusail Iconic Stadium, Lusail – 24 November 2022

Brazil opened their tournament with a controlled performance, eventually breaking the deadlock after a goalless first half. The breakthrough came in the 62nd minute when Richarlison reacted quickest to a rebound inside the penalty area, finishing low past the Serbian goalkeeper to give Brazil the lead.

Brazil continued to dominate possession and created several chances as the match progressed, and they doubled their advantage in the 73rd minute. Richarlison scored his second goal of the game with an acrobatic finish after controlling a pass from Vinícius Júnior, extending Brazil’s lead to 2–0.

Serbia struggled to create clear opportunities and were unable to test Brazil’s defence consistently. Brazil maintained control of the match in the closing stages to secure a comfortable group stage win.

Key events:

62′ – Richarlison (Brazil) goal.

73′ – Richarlison (Brazil) goal (acrobatic finish).

Brazil’s victory in their opening group match set a positive tone for their World Cup campaign.
