England 6–2 Iran
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group B, Khalifa International Stadium, Doha – 21 November 2022

England began their 2022 World Cup campaign with a sensational attacking performance and a high-scoring victory over Iran. The deadlock was broken in the 35th minute when Jude Bellingham rose to head home following a well-timed cross, putting the Three Lions ahead. England doubled their lead in the 43rd minute through Bukayo Saka, who finished expertly from inside the box before half-time became 3–0 with a 45th-minute strike from Raheem Sterling.

In the second half, England continued to press and extend their lead. Saka scored his second in the 62nd minute with a composed low finish, and Marcus Rashford added a fifth in the 71st minute after quickly turning on a pass inside the area. Jack Grealish wrapped up England’s scoring in the 90th minute with a close-range finish.

Iran did hit back on two occasions. Mehdi Taremi pulled a goal back in the 65th minute with a well-placed strike, and he later converted a 90+13th-minute penalty to make the scoreline 6–2, but England’s dominance was already established.
