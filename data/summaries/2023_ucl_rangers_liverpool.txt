Rangers 1–1 Liverpool
2022–23 UEFA Champions League – Group A, Ibrox Stadium, Glasgow – 4 October 2022

Rangers and Liverpool played out a hard-fought draw in Group A, with both teams taking a point in a competitive clash at Ibrox. The match was closely contested from the start, with Liverpool enjoying more possession and probing Rangers’ defence, while the Scottish hosts remained organised and disciplined in their shape. The breakthrough came in the 25th minute, when Sadio Mané finished well inside the box after good approach play from Liverpool’s forward line, giving the visitors the lead. Rangers responded and gradually grew into the game, creating half chances and pressuring Liverpool’s back line with direct play and set pieces. (skysports.com￼)

Rangers’ persistence paid off before half-time. In the 42nd minute, Danilo Lazović fired home from close range after latching onto a loose ball in the penalty area to level the score, energising the home crowd. The second half remained competitive, with both sides having opportunities to win it, but neither could find a second goal. Rangers defended resolutely to preserve the draw, while Liverpool’s attempts to regain the lead were thwarted by timely interventions and saves. (skysports.com￼)
