Serbia 0–2 England
2026 FIFA World Cup Qualifier – UEFA Group K, Wembley Stadium, London – 13 November 2025

England secured a 2–0 victory over Serbia in their World Cup qualifying fixture at Wembley, maintaining a perfect record in Group K. After a competitive start, England broke the deadlock in the 28th minute when Bukayo Saka volleyed home from close range following sustained pressure and good build-up play in the attacking third. England maintained control for much of the first half and limited Serbia to few clear goalscoring opportunities.  ￼

Despite Serbia pushing to get back into the match after the break, England’s defence remained organised and resilient. The victory was sealed late on when Eberechi Eze curled a finish into the top corner in the 90th minute, extending England’s lead and ensuring all three points.  ￼

Key events:
	•	28′ – Bukayo Saka (England) goal.  ￼
	•	90′ – Eberechi Eze (England) goal.  ￼

England’s win kept them unbeaten and clean-sheeted in their qualifying campaign.
