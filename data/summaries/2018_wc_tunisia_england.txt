Tunisia 1–2 England
2018 FIFA World Cup – Group G, Volgograd Arena, Volgograd – 18 June 2018

England opened their World Cup campaign with a late victory over Tunisia. England took the lead in the 11th minute when Harry Kane scored from close range after a rebound off a header inside the penalty area, capitalising on early pressure.

Tunisia responded before half-time, earning a penalty in the 35th minute after Kyle Walker fouled in the area. Ferjani Sassi converted the spot-kick to level the score at 1–1.

The match remained tight after the interval, with England dominating possession and creating several opportunities but unable to break through Tunisia’s defence. In stoppage time, England secured the win as Kane headed home from a 91st-minute corner, completing his brace and earning England all three points.
