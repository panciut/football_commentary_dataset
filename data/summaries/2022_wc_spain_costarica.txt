Spain 7–0 Costa Rica
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group E, Al Thumama Stadium, Doha – 23 November 2022

Spain produced a commanding and historic performance against Costa Rica in their World Cup group match, dominating from the outset and recording one of the largest victories of the tournament. After settling into rhythm early, Spain opened the scoring in the 11th minute when Dani Olmo finished smartly after a composed team move put him through on goal, giving La Roja the lead. The pressure quickly mounted, and in the 21st minute Marco Asensio doubled the advantage with a low finish from inside the box after linking with Jordi Alba down the left flank. Spain were awarded a penalty in the 31st minute when Oscar Duarte fouled a Spanish attacker, and Ferran Torres converted calmly from the spot to make it 3–0 at half-time. Torres added his second of the night early in the second half with a composed strike in the 54th minute to extend the lead further. (turn0search2

Spain continued to press throughout the remainder of the match. In the 74th minute, Gavi struck with a precise volley to make it 5–0, becoming one of the youngest goalscorers in World Cup history. Late goals from Carlos Soler in the 90th minute and Álvaro Morata in injury time completed the dominant 7–0 scoreline, as Costa Rica were unable to mount any sustained threat and finished without a single shot on target.
