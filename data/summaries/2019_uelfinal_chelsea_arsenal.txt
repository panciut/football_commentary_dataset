Chelsea 4–1 Arsenal
2019 UEFA Europa League Final, Baku Olympic Stadium, Baku – 29 May 2019

Chelsea dominated the final after a scoreless first half and took the lead soon after the break in the 49th minute when Olivier Giroud headed home from close range following a low cross into the box.

Chelsea extended their advantage in the 60th minute through Pedro, who steered a low finish into the corner after being set up by Eden Hazard. Ten minutes later, Chelsea were awarded a penalty after Ainsley Maitland-Niles fouled Giroud in the box; Hazard converted the spot-kick calmly, sending the goalkeeper the wrong way to make it 3–0.

Arsenal pulled a goal back in the 69th minute with a powerful right-footed strike from substitute Alex Iwobi, giving the Gunners a brief spark. However, Chelsea restored their three-goal cushion just three minutes later, with Hazard netting again from inside the box after a pass from Giroud to make it 4–1, sealing a convincing victory.

Chelsea’s second-half scoring burst secured the Europa League title with a comprehensive 4–1 win over Arsenal.
