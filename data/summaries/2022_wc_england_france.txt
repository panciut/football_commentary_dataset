England 1–2 France
2022 FIFA World Cup – Quarter-Final, Al Bayt Stadium, Al Khor – 10 December 2022

France took the lead in the 17th minute when Aurélien Tchouaméni unleashed a powerful shot from distance that beat England goalkeeper Jordan Pickford, giving Les Bleus an early advantage.

England responded after the break, earning a penalty in the 54th minute when Bukayo Saka was fouled in the box. Harry Kane stepped up and converted from the spot to level the score, marking a significant moment in the match.

The decisive goal arrived in the 78th minute as Olivier Giroud headed France back in front from close range following a well-worked attack. Although England were awarded another penalty late on, Kane’s spot kick was struck over the bar, leaving France in control.

England pushed for an equaliser in the closing stages, with Harry Maguire coming close with a header that struck the post and Marcus Rashford firing a late free kick narrowly over the bar.

Key events:

17′ – Aurélien Tchouaméni (France) goal.

54′ – Harry Kane (England) penalty goal.

78′ – Olivier Giroud (France) goal.

France’s 2–1 win sent the defending champions into the semi-finals, while England’s World Cup run ended at the quarter-final stage.
