Croatia 2–4 England
UEFA Euro 2004 – Group B, Estádio da Luz, Lisbon – 21 June 2004

England recovered from an early deficit to secure a 4–2 victory over Croatia in their final Group B match at UEFA Euro 2004, ensuring progression to the quarter-finals with a decisive attacking display.

Croatia struck first in the 6th minute when Niko Kovač capitalised on a set-piece situation and finished low past England’s goalkeeper to give the hosts an early lead. England responded before half-time, drawing level in the 40th minute when Paul Scholes headed in from close range after a well-worked move. Just before the break, Wayne Rooney put England ahead with a powerful strike in stoppage time, giving them a 2–1 advantage at the interval.

In the second half, Rooney extended England’s lead further with a composed finish in the 68th minute following an incisive run and chance created by the forwards. Croatia pulled a goal back through Igor Tudor in the 74th minute, reducing the deficit with a well-timed finish inside the box. However, England restored their two-goal cushion when Frank Lampard converted in the 79th minute after sustained pressure in the attacking third, sealing a 4–2 victory.

The match featured a strong attacking response from England after falling behind early, with goals from Scholes, Rooney (two), and Lampard overturning Croatia’s initial advantage and securing qualification to the tournament’s knockout stage.
