Germany 4–1 England
2010 FIFA World Cup – Round of 16, Free State Stadium, Bloemfontein – 27 June 2010

Germany opened the scoring in the 20th minute when Miroslav Klose capitalised on a long goal kick and finished past England’s goalkeeper.
Lukas Podolski extended Germany’s lead in the 32nd minute with a low finish after a well-worked move.

England responded in the 37th minute, with Matthew Upson heading home from a Steven Gerrard cross to reduce the deficit to 2–1. Shortly after, Frank Lampard struck the ball over the line from distance, but the referee did not award a goal despite replays showing it had crossed the line.

In the second half, Thomas Müller turned the match decisively in Germany’s favour by scoring twice in quick succession in the 67th and 70th minutes, sealing a comprehensive victory and eliminating England from the tournament.
