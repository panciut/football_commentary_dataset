Brighton & Hove Albion 2–2 Manchester United (after extra time)
1983 FA Cup Final, Wembley Stadium, London – 21 May 1983

The 1983 FA Cup Final between Brighton & Hove Albion and Manchester United ended in a dramatic 2–2 draw, forcing a replay to decide the trophy. Brighton took the lead in the 27th minute when Gordon Smith finished clinically after a quick attacking move. Manchester United responded before half-time, equalising in the 35th minute through Frank Stapleton, who headed home from close range.

United went ahead in the second half in the 55th minute when Ray Wilkins struck a powerful long-range shot into the net, seemingly putting Manchester United on course for victory. However, Brighton refused to concede defeat and levelled the match in the 87th minute through Gary Stevens, whose fierce shot beat the goalkeeper and sent the final into extra time.

Extra time produced no further goals, but the match is remembered for Brighton’s famous late chance in the final seconds, when Gordon Smith was denied by a crucial save from Gary Bailey, preserving the draw. With the score still 2–2 after 120 minutes, the FA Cup Final went to a replay.
