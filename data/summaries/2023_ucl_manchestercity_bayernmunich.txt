Manchester City 3–0 Bayern Munich (first leg)
2022–23 UEFA Champions League – Quarter-Final, Etihad Stadium, Manchester – 11 April 2023

Manchester City established a strong advantage in the quarter-final tie with a commanding performance at home. City took the lead in the 27th minute when Rodri curled a stunning long-range strike into the top corner to break the deadlock. After a competitive but scoreless period following the opener, City extended their advantage in the 70th minute as Bernardo Silva headed in from close range after a well-worked move. Erling Haaland sealed the convincing 3–0 scoreline in the 76th minute, finishing low inside the box following another sustained City attack. City’s dominant display gave them a substantial cushion going into the second leg.

Bayern Munich 1–1 Manchester City (second leg)
Champions League Quarter-Final, Allianz Arena, Munich – 19 April 2023

In the return leg, Bayern pushed for a response but were unable to overturn the first-leg deficit. Erling Haaland struck first for City in the 57th minute, finishing a counter-attack to extend his fine scoring run. Bayern pulled one back in the 83rd minute when Joshua Kimmich converted a penalty, but the Panthers’ late goal was not enough to shift the tie. The match ended 1–1, and Manchester City advanced 4–1 on aggregate to reach the Champions League semi-finals.
