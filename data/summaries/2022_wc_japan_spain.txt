Japan 2–1 Spain
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group E, Khalifa International Stadium, Doha – 1 December 2022

Japan pulled off another shock result in Qatar by coming from behind to beat Spain and top Group E. Spain took the lead in the 11th minute when Álvaro Morata headed in from close range after a well-worked move, giving La Roja early control of the match.

The match turned shortly after half-time. In the 48th minute, Ritsu Doan struck from outside the box with a powerful left-footed shot to level the score. Just minutes later, in the 51st minute, Ao Tanaka finished from close range after a cut-back into the box, putting Japan ahead and completing the turnaround.

Japan’s second-half resurgence stunned the tournament favourites, with the winning goal confirmed after a VAR check ruled the play legitimate. The result secured Japan top spot in the group and knocked Germany out, while Spain progressed as runners-up.
