France 2–1 Denmark
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group D, Stadium 974, Doha – 26 November 2022

France secured a hard-fought victory over Denmark to become the first team to qualify for the Round of 16 at the 2022 FIFA World Cup. The match was tightly contested for much of the first half, but France eventually broke through after the break. Kylian Mbappé opened the scoring in the 61st minute, finishing from close range after linking play on the left flank.

Denmark responded seven minutes later. In the 68th minute, Andreas Christensen headed in from a corner after sustained pressure on France’s defence, levelling the score and breathing new life into the Danes’ campaign.

The decisive goal came late in the match. In the 86th minute, Mbappé struck again, meeting Antoine Griezmann’s cross with his thigh to restore France’s lead and secure the 2–1 win.

France controlled much of the possession and created several chances throughout the game, while Denmark showed resilience and threatened on set pieces, but could not find a late equaliser.

Key events:

61′ – Kylian Mbappé (France) goal.

68′ – Andreas Christensen (Denmark) goal.

86′ – Kylian Mbappé (France) goal.

France’s win maintained their unbeaten start to the tournament and ensured early progression to the knockout stage.
