Netherlands 1–2 England
UEFA Euro 2024 – Semi-Final, Signal Iduna Park, Dortmund – 10 July 2024

England advanced to the Euro 2024 final with a dramatic come-from-behind win against the Netherlands. Xavi Simons put the Dutch ahead early, scoring in the 7th minute with a long-range effort that beat Jordan Pickford after winning possession high up the pitch. 

England responded in the 18th minute when Harry Kane converted from the penalty spot after he was fouled inside the box, bringing the score level and shifting momentum toward the Three Lions. England dominated possession for much of the first half and created several attacking opportunities, though both sides had limited clear chances after the equaliser. 

With the match heading toward extra time, Ollie Watkins, who had come on as a substitute, scored a dramatic winner in the 90th minute. Watkins controlled a pass from Cole Palmer and fired low into the far corner, securing a 2–1 victory and sending England into the Euro 2024 final.
