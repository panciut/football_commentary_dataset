England 6–1 Panama
2018 FIFA World Cup – Group G, Nizhny Novgorod Stadium, Nizhny Novgorod – 24 June 2018

England produced a commanding performance against Panama to record one of their biggest World Cup victories. The opening goal came in the 8th minute, when John Stones rose to head home from a Kieran Trippier corner, giving England an early advantage.

In the 22nd minute, Harry Kane converted a penalty after Jesse Lingard was brought down in the box, extending England’s lead. Moments later, Jesse Lingard scored a superb curling effort from outside the box in the 36th minute, furthering England’s dominance.

Before half-time, Stones added his second goal with another header in the 40th minute, making it 4–0. Kane then converted a second penalty in first-half added time, boosting England’s tally before the break.

In the second half, Kane completed his hat-trick in the 62nd minute when his shot, deflected off his heel, looped into the net. Panama’s Felipe Baloy scored a historic goal in the 78th minute, earning his side their first ever World Cup finals goal.
