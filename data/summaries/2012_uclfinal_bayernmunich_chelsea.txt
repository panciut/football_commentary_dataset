Bayern Munich 1–1 Chelsea (Chelsea win 4–3 on penalties)
2012 UEFA Champions League Final, Allianz Arena, Munich – 19 May 2012

Bayern Munich dominated possession and territory for most of the final, pressing Chelsea deep into their own half and creating the majority of chances. The breakthrough came in the 83rd minute, when Thomas Müller headed Bayern into the lead from close range after sustained pressure.

Chelsea responded almost immediately. From their first corner of the match, Didier Drogba rose highest to score a powerful header in the 88th minute, levelling the match and forcing extra time.

In extra time, Bayern were awarded a penalty in the 95th minute following a foul on Franck Ribéry, but Arjen Robben’s spot-kick was saved by Petr Čech. With no further goals, the final went to a penalty shoot-out. Chelsea converted four penalties, while Čech saved attempts from Ivica Olić and Bastian Schweinsteiger. Drogba scored the decisive penalty to secure Chelsea’s first Champions League title.

Key events:

83′ – Thomas Müller (Bayern Munich) goal.

88′ – Didier Drogba (Chelsea) goal.

95′ – Arjen Robben penalty saved by Petr Čech.

Penalty shoot-out: Chelsea win 4–3.
