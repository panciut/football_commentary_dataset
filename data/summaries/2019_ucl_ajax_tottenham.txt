Ajax 2–3 Tottenham Hotspur
2018–19 UEFA Champions League – Semi-final (second leg), Johan Cruyff Arena, Amsterdam – 8 May 2019
Tottenham advanced to the final on away goals with a dramatic comeback after trailing on aggregate.

Ajax took early control, opening the scoring in the 5th minute when Matthijs de Ligt headed in from a corner to put the hosts ahead. A strong first-half display continued when Hakim Ziyech added a second for Ajax in the 35th minute with a curled finish, leaving Ajax leading 2–0 on the night and 3–0 on aggregate.

Tottenham responded after half-time through Lucas Moura, who pulled one back in the 55th minute, finishing low inside the box following sustained pressure. Moura struck again in the 59th minute, tapping in from close range after Ajax failed to clear a danger, reducing the deficit and shifting momentum sharply toward Spurs.

With the aggregate score poised at 3–2 to Ajax, Tottenham pressed for a winner. In the 90+6th minute, Moura completed his hat-trick by turning the ball into the net from close range after a late attacking move, securing a stunning 3–2 win on the night and a 3–3 aggregate, sending Tottenham into their first Champions League final on away goals.
