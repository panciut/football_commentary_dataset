Milan 3–3 Liverpool (Liverpool win 3–2 on penalties)
2005 UEFA Champions League Final, Atatürk Olympic Stadium, Istanbul – 25 May 2005

AC Milan started the final strongly, taking the lead in the 1st minute through Paolo Maldini’s strike from a set-piece delivery.
Hernán Crespo extended Milan’s advantage before half-time with two goals in the 39th and 44th minutes, leaving the Italian side 3–0 up at the break.

Liverpool staged one of the most remarkable comebacks in football history early in the second half: Steven Gerrard headed in to make it 3–1 in the 54th minute,
Vladimír Šmicer struck from distance in the 56th minute,
and Xabi Alonso converted a rebound after his penalty was initially saved to complete the comeback in the 61st minute, leveling the score at 3–3.

The match remained 3–3 after extra time, leading to a penalty shoot-out. Liverpool goalkeeper Jerzy Dudek produced crucial saves, including stopping Andrea Pirlo and Andriy Shevchenko, while Milan’s Serginho missed his spot-kick. Liverpool prevailed 3–2 on penalties, securing their fifth European Cup.
