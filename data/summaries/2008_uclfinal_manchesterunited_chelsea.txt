Manchester United 1–1 Chelsea (Manchester United win 6–5 on penalties)
2008 UEFA Champions League Final, Luzhniki Stadium, Moscow – 21 May 2008

Manchester United opened the scoring in the 26th minute as Cristiano Ronaldo headed in from a Wes Brown cross to put United ahead in the first half.
Chelsea responded just before half-time, with Frank Lampard finishing from close range to level the score 1–1 entering the break.

The second half and extra time saw few clear chances, with both defences maintaining parity. Late in extra time, Chelsea’s Didier Drogba was sent off for violent conduct, reducing the Blues to ten men.

The match went to a penalty shoot-out. Cristiano Ronaldo missed United’s third kick, and Chelsea captain John Terry had the opportunity to win the title with the fifth spot-kick but slipped and hit the post.
In sudden death, Manchester United prevailed 6–5 on penalties, securing the club’s third European Cup.
