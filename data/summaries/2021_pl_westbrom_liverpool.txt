West Bromwich Albion 1–2 Liverpool
2020–21 Premier League – The Hawthorns, West Bromwich – 16 May 2021

West Brom took the lead against the run of play in the 15th minute when Hal Robson-Kanu curled a precise finish past Liverpool’s goalkeeper after a swift move into the box.

Liverpool responded before half-time. In the 33rd minute, Mohamed Salah struck with a low curling shot from outside the area to level the scores, restoring parity heading into the break.

For much of the second half, both teams had opportunities, including chances for Liverpool to take the lead, but the match appeared to be heading for a draw. In the 90+5th minute, Liverpool’s goalkeeper Alisson Becker rose for a corner and headed the decisive goal into the top corner, completing a dramatic late winner and securing all three points for the visitors.

Key events:

15′ – Hal Robson-Kanu (West Bromwich Albion) goal.

33′ – Mohamed Salah (Liverpool) goal.

90+5′ – Alisson Becker (Liverpool) goal (header).

Liverpool’s stoppage-time winner kept their push for European qualification alive with a dramatic conclusion at The Hawthorns.
