Arsenal 1–2 Liverpool
2001 FA Cup Final, Millennium Stadium, Cardiff – 12 May 2001

Liverpool completed a dramatic late comeback to win the 2001 FA Cup Final against Arsenal. Arsenal opened the scoring in the 72nd minute when Freddie Ljungberg finished after good build-up play to put the Gunners ahead. Arsenal maintained the lead for much of the match and created several chances, but could not extend it despite pressure from Liverpool.  ￼

Liverpool responded late. In the 83rd minute, Michael Owen equalised after Arsenal failed to clear a free-kick, pouncing on the loose ball and finishing from inside the box to level the score. Owen then completed the turnaround in the 88th minute, racing clear after a long pass from Patrik Berger and finishing low past the keeper to give Liverpool the lead.  ￼

The match ended 2–1 to Liverpool, securing the FA Cup with two late goals that overturned Arsenal’s advantage in one of the competition’s most memorable finals.  ￼
