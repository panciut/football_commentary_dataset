England 3–0 Senegal
2022 FIFA World Cup – Round of 16, Al Bayt Stadium, Al Khor – 4 December 2022
England progressed to the World Cup quarter-finals with a convincing victory over Senegal. After a relatively even opening phase, the breakthrough came in the 39th minute when Jordan Henderson finished low from close range following a well-worked move that began with pressure in midfield, giving England the lead before half-time. 
Just before the break, in 45+3′, Harry Kane extended England’s advantage. A rapid counter-attack saw Jude Bellingham drive forward and lay the ball into space for Kane, who fired past goalkeeper Édouard Mendy for his first goal of the tournament. 
Senegal attempted to respond after the interval, but England controlled the match and continued to threaten on the break. In the 57th minute, Bukayo Saka added a third with a composed finish from inside the box after linking with Phil Foden, effectively sealing the win and England’s place in the quarter-finals. 
Key events:


39′ – Jordan Henderson (England) goal. 


45+3′ – Harry Kane (England) goal. 


57′ – Bukayo Saka (England) goal. 


England’s victory set up a quarter-final tie against France.
