Spain 1–1 Germany
2022 FIFA World Cup – Group E, Al Bayt Stadium, Al Khor – 27 November 2022

Spain and Germany shared the points in a high-quality Group E encounter that kept the group wide open. Spain dominated possession in the first half and looked the more comfortable side on the ball, creating early chances through quick combinations and wide overloads. Dani Olmo came closest before the break, forcing a strong save and striking the crossbar with a powerful effort from distance.

Germany improved after half-time, increasing their tempo and pressing higher up the pitch, but Spain struck first in the 62nd minute. Substitute Álvaro Morata reacted quickest to a low cut-back inside the box, finishing at the near post to give Spain the lead.

Germany responded with urgency, pushing numbers forward and creating sustained pressure around Spain’s penalty area. Their persistence paid off in the 83rd minute, when substitute Niclas Füllkrug controlled the ball inside the box and fired a low shot past the goalkeeper to level the score.

The closing minutes were intense, with both sides searching for a winner, but neither could find a decisive breakthrough. The 1–1 draw left qualification to be decided on the final matchday.
